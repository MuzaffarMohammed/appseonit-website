Types of e-commerce

As commerce continues to grow, so the different ways that it is managed. Following are the most common set of e-commerce models:

Business to Consumer (B2C):

Business to Consumer e-commerce is the most desired e-commerce model. Business to consumer means that the sale is taking place between a business and a consumer, in other ways B2C, is a business model in which a company sells a service or a product directly to a consumer. like when you buy a something from an online seller.

Business to Business (B2B):

Business to Business (B2B) is a sales model that involves one business selling products or services to other businesses. This business deal is completely opposite to business-to-consumer sales where a business sells products or services to consumers.

Direct to Consumer (D2C):

Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) is the latest model of ecommerce. where producers or manufacturers are selling there products or services directly to their end customer without going through traditional process like manufacturer> to a wholesaler then to a distributor > then to a retailers and finally to a consumer.

Consumer to Consumer (C2C):

Consumer to Consumer e-commerce refers to the business deal of a goods or services between two or more consumers.

Consumer to Business (C2B):

Consumer to business is a type of business model in which the consumer provides a service or product to the business organization and this is completely opposite of business-to-consumer (B2C) where a company provides a service to consumer with sales of goods and services.
